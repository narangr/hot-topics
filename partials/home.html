THE VALUE OF MONEY

Money is a medium of exchange that facilitates trade in goods and services. From centuries “money” has always found its place in civilizations all over the world, in hugely similar and different forms. Wherever people pro¬gressed beyond simple barter, they began to use their most market¬able goods as a medium for exchange. In primitive societies, they used cattle, or measures of grain, salt, or fish. In early civilizations where the division of labour extended to larger areas, gold or silver emerged as the most marketable good and finally as the only medi¬um of exchange, called money. It is obvious that the leaders of the world did not invent the use of money. But they frequently usurped control over it whenever they suffered budget deficits and could gain revenue from currency debasement. When an economic good is sought and wanted, not only for its use in consumption or production but also for purposes of exchange, to be held in reserve for later ex¬changes, the demand for it obvi¬ously increases. People seek money because it has purchasing power, and part of this purchasing power is gen¬erated by the people’s demand for money.(The Source)

GMO FOOD

GMO foods are genetically modified foods, also known as genetically engineered food. These foods are produced from organisms that have been introduced into their DNA using the method of genetic engineering. It benefits the farmers as it increases crop yield, resistance to insects and tolerance for heat, cold, or drought. Along with some benefits, it carries some disadvantages like toxicity, inherently insatiable, can cause allergic reaction, can suppress the immune system & further more. Now you will be wondering how GMO work in food? So, genetic modification allows just one individual gene or a small number of genes, to be inserted into plants or animals. This enables them to be used in new & very precise ways. Compiling all pros & cons, we can conclude that GMO foods are helpful to human beings but should be used upto a certain extent. Also, GM food helps us to find sustainable ways to feed people. In countries that lack access to nutrient-rich foods, GMO foods are really helpful. The crops have longer shelf life that allow them to be shipped to remote areas.(The source)
